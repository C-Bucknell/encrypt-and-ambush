ENCRYPT&AMBUSH / HOW TO PLAY

How to play — step by step

Goal: finish with the most points. Points = every square you control on the map, +1 for every enemy order you successfully crack.

Step 1 — Join & set up

Click Join a team on the main menu — you'll get a random codename and colour automatically, no choice needed. In the Setup panel, enter a Caesar cipher shift (any number 1–25) and click Save. Do this before the teacher starts the game — your shift locks in the moment it does and can't be changed afterwards.

Step 2 — Learn the board

Squares are named by column (A–F) then row (1–5) — A1 is top-left, F5 is bottom-right. When you MOVE, you can only move to a square directly next to yours:

Only up / down / left / right count as adjacent — the diagonal squares (marked ✕) don't count, even though they look close on the map.

Step 3 — Write your order

Each round, every team submits exactly one order. There are two kinds:

MOVE — claim a square

AMBUSH — strike a rival agency

RUBY

the target's 4-letter codename — not their colour word

Codenames (like RUBY, AQUA, LIME) are always 4 letters. Check the legend under your tactical map any time you need a reminder of which codename belongs to which team.

Step 4 — Submit, then wait

Type your order, check the encrypted version that appears next to it, then click Submit encrypted order. There's no undo after that — double-check your squares and codename spelling before you submit.

Watch the Incoming message panel on your screen — when it lights up and it isn't your own order, type your best guess of the plain text. Guess right and you score a point; guess wrong and nothing happens, no penalty.

Step 6 — Scoring & next round

Your score = every square you control + 1 point for every message you've successfully cracked. This repeats for several rounds, then the game ends and the highest score wins.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • MOVE must start from a square you already control — not just any square.
  • Adjacent means up/down/left/right only — never diagonal, even if it looks close.
  • AMBUSH targets the enemy's codename (e.g. RUBY), not their colour word (e.g. "Red").
  • Set your cipher shift before the game starts — it locks in and can't be changed once it does.
  • One order per round, with no undo — check squares and spelling before you hit submit.
  • During the crack window, you're guessing someone else's message — you can't crack your own.
